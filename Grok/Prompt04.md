**Entry-level certifications in cloud computing** are foundational or associate-level credentials that validate basic knowledge of cloud concepts, services, and platforms. They help candidates stand out for roles like Cloud Support Associate, Junior Cloud Engineer, Cloud Administrator, or Operations roles, especially when paired with hands-on projects.

These certs typically require little to no prior experience and can be prepared for in weeks to a couple of months with self-study.

**Most recommended starting points**:
- **AWS Cloud Practitioner** — The most popular entry cert due to AWS's market dominance. It teaches universal cloud concepts while introducing the leading platform.
- **Azure AZ-900** — Excellent if targeting Microsoft-heavy companies (common in enterprises and government).
- Many advise starting with one foundational cert, then moving to an **Associate-level** (e.g., AWS Solutions Architect Associate, Azure Administrator AZ-104, or Google Associate Cloud Engineer) for stronger job prospects.

### Why These Certs Matter for Entry-Level Jobs
- They demonstrate commitment and basic competency without needing years of experience.
- Employers often list them (or equivalent knowledge) in job postings for support, junior engineer, and admin roles.
- Foundational certs alone may not land senior roles but help you pass initial screens and build toward better-paying positions.
- Pair them with **free-tier practice** on the respective platforms and GitHub projects (e.g., deploy a simple web app, set up storage, or automate basic tasks).

### Preparation Tips
- **Time investment**: 2–8 weeks of consistent study (10–20 hours/week) for foundational certs.
- **Resources**: Official free training (AWS Skill Builder, Microsoft Learn, Google Cloud Skills Boost), A Cloud Guru, Coursera, Whizlabs, or Udemy practice exams.
- Focus on understanding concepts over memorization — exams test practical awareness.
- No strict prerequisites, but basic IT knowledge (networking, servers) helps.

**Pro Tip**: Choose based on job market in your area or target companies. AWS generally offers the widest opportunities, Azure shines in corporate settings, and GCP in data/AI-focused roles. Many professionals get 1–2 foundational certs then specialize.
