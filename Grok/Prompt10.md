**The global cloud computing market in 2026 is valued at approximately $900 billion to $1.04 trillion**, depending on the source and exact scope (e.g., public cloud services vs. broader including private/hybrid). It is on track to surpass **$1 trillion** in 2026 and is projected to grow rapidly in the coming years, driven by AI integration, digital transformation, hybrid/multi-cloud adoption, and data center expansion.

### Current Market Size (2026 Estimates)
Different reputable research firms report figures in this range (variations stem from definitions: some focus on public cloud end-user spending, others include broader services):

- **Fortune Business Insights**: $905.33 billion in 2026 (up from $781.27 billion in 2025).
- **Other sources**: Around $917–$1,040 billion, with several projecting it will cross the $1 trillion mark during the year.
- **Public cloud infrastructure services** (IaaS/PaaS/SaaS subset): On track to exceed $500 billion in spending for the first time in 2026.

**Note**: The U.S. accounts for a large share (often ~50%+ of the global market), with strong growth in North America.

### Growth Projections
The market is expected to more than double or triple by the early 2030s, with CAGRs typically in the 12–20% range.

- Growth is fueled heavily by **AI/ML workloads**, hyperscaler investments, edge computing, and enterprise migrations.
- Cloud infrastructure spending (a key subset) is seeing particularly strong growth, with quarterly figures already in the $120B+ range in early 2026.

### Key Drivers and Context
- **Dominant players**: AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud continue to lead (combined ~65–70%+ market share in infrastructure), with strong Q1 2026 revenue growth.
- **Regional strength**: North America leads; Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region in many forecasts.
- **Related spending**: Broader IT spending (including cloud) is projected around $6.3 trillion in 2026, with data centers and AI infrastructure seeing outsized growth.

**In short**: The cloud market is already massive (~$900B–$1T in 2026) and is in a high-growth phase, with strong projections toward $2–3 trillion by the early 2030s. These figures can vary by methodology, so treat them as directional benchmarks from major analysts. AI demand is a major accelerator for future expansion.
